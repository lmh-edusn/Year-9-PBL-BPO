Take Action to Restore Snub-nosed Monkey

Please tell Congress that you support the Burmese snub-nosed monkey Management Act to revive and preserve Indigenous cultures, Tribal landscapes, and iconic wildlife.

Take Action to Restore Snub-nosed Monkey

1. Support Conservation Organizations
  • Donate to our organizations work to protect snub-nosed monkey habitats and combat threats like poaching and deforestation.
  • Volunteer your time to conservation efforts, if possible. This could involve participating in habitat restoration projects or wildlife monitoring programs.
2. Educate Yourself and Others
  • Learn about the threats facing snub-nosed monkeys and the importance of their conservation.
  • Share information about the importance of buffalo restoration with your friends and family.
  • Support responsible ecotourism that contributes to conservation efforts and minimizes negative impacts on wildlife and habitats.
3. Reduce Your Carbon Footprint
  • Climate change is a significant threat to snub-nosed monkeys and their habitats. Reduce your carbon footprint by conserving energy, reducing waste, and choosing sustainable transportation options.
  • Support policies that promote renewable energy and sustainable practices.
4. Advocate for Strong Conservation Laws
  • Contact your elected officials and urge them to support strong laws protecting snub-nosed monkeys and their habitats.
  • Support policies that promote sustainable land use and protect forests.

Speak Out to Restore Grassland Habitat

Tell your community members of Congress to fully support and help archive the North American, Southeast Asia, and Africa Grasslands Conservation Act.

Speak Out to Restore Grassland Habitat

1. Educate Yourself and Others
  • Learn about the importance of grasslands for biodiversity, climate regulation, and ecosystem services.
  • Share your knowledge with friends, family, and community members through conversations, social media, or presentations.
  • Support our organizations to act grassland conservation and restoration.
2. Support Grassland Conservation Organizations
  • Donate to ourorganizations or local conservation groups working to protect and restore grasslands
  • Volunteer your time to participate in grassland restoration projects or outreach activities.
3. Advocate for Policy Changes
  • Contact your elected representatives to express your support for policies that protect and restore grasslands.
  • Urge policymakers to support conservation programs, sustainable land use practices, and incentives for farmers and ranchers to adopt grassland-friendly practices.
  • Participate in public hearings and comment on proposed regulations that affect grasslands.
4. Support Sustainable Agriculture
  • Choose to purchase products from farmers and ranchers who use sustainable grazing practices that promote grassland health.
  • Support initiatives that promote rotational grazing, cover cropping, and other practices that improve soil health and biodiversity.
5. Organize Community Events
  • Host community events, such as nature walks, workshops, or film screenings, to raise awareness about grassland conservation.
  • Collaborate with local schools and organizations to educate the next generation about the importance of grasslands.

Take Action for Nature and Clean Energy

Please urge Congress and your governor to invest in climate solutions to recover wildlife habitat and make our communities more resilient to climate change.

Take Action for Nature and Clean Energy

1. Reduce your carbon footprint
  • Turn off lights, unplug electronics, and adjust your thermostat.
  • Take shorter showers, fix leaks, and water your lawn efficiently.
  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle. Compost food scraps.
  • Walk, bike, carpool, or use public transportation.
2. Support clean energy
  • Switch to a utility that sources energy from renewable sources like wind or solar.
  • Look for ENERGY STAR-certified products.
3. Educate yourself and others
  • Learn about climate change and environmental issues.
  • Share information with friends and family.
  • Volunteer with environmental organizations.
4. Participate in community actions
  • Organize clean-up events and tree-planting initiatives.
  • Support local businesses that prioritize sustainability.
5. Join environmental organizations
  • Participate in local and global initiatives.
  • Donate to organizations working on climate change and environmental conservation.

Responsible for Clean Zombie Mines

Tell your federal legislators to support zombie mine prevention bills to ensure coal companies not the people are responsible for mine clean up and the impact on enviromental crisis.

Responsible for Clean Zombie Mines

1. Volunteer with Mine Clearance Organizations
  • Organizations like the Mine Action Group of Myanmar (MAG) and Halo Trust work to clear landmines and educate communities about the dangers. Volunteering with them can directly contribute to mine clearance efforts.
  • Many local organizations are working on mine clearance and victim assistance. Consider donating to or volunteering with these organizations.
  • Raise awareness about the landmine problem in Myanmar through social media, community events, or by contacting your local representatives.
2. Advocacy for Clean Zombie Mines
  • Write to your government representatives urging them to support mine clearance efforts in Myanmar and to pressure the Myanmar military to cease the use of landmines.
  • Support international organizations like the United Nations and the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) in their efforts to address the landmine issue globally.

Keep salmon recovery flowing

We need our leaders to act quickly to modernize our energy and transportation services, and restore abundant wild salmon to the Columbia River Basin for the better water quality.

Keep salmon recovery flowing

1. Reduce Your Carbon Footprint
  • Climate change is a major threat to salmon populations.bReduce your carbon footprint by conserving energy, reducing waste, and choosing sustainable transportation options.
  • Water is essential for salmon habitat. Conserve water by taking shorter showers, fixing leaks, and watering your lawn efficiently.
  • Choose sustainably sourced seafood and avoid overfished species.
2. Community Actions
  • Volunteer with organizations working to restore salmon habitat, monitor fish populations, or remove invasive species.
  • Attend local events focused on salmon conservation and restoration.
  • Support businesses that prioritize sustainability and environmental protection.
3. Advocacy
  • Contact your local, state, and federal representatives to urge them to support policies that protect salmon and their habitat.
  • Donate to organizations working to protect salmon and their ecosystems.
  • Research the specific threats facing salmon in your region and take action accordingly.
